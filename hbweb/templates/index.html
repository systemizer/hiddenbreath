HiddenBreath

HiddenBreath is a servce that allows you to share messages and data in the situation you are unable to do so yourself. This service itself should not be trusted; this merely routes encrypted messages on your behalf. This means that all messages and data uploaded should be encrypted, and you should provide your recipients with secret keys via an alternative medium.

How it works

I will monitor your public social feeds on the internet (i.e. twitter, facebook, instagram, etc). If I don't see that you've posted a message in the past month, I will call you over the phone (with a number you've provided). This number should be a personal number so I can verify whether to disseminate your encrypted messages. If I fail to reach you after 3 attempts (at random intervals over the month following your inactivity), I will automatically send your encrypted messages to recipients you have previously specified.

Questions you might have

Why shouldn't I trust this service?

As far as you are aware, I am a stranger; you cannot trust me. This is why you must encrypt your messages so I can't read them. If you upload a message that I think is not properly encrypted, I will not accept it.

How do I encrypt messages?

This is where you need to be careful. If your encryption scheme is not secure, someone could brute force the decryption. If you use a correct scheme, but the software you use to encrypt is not trusted, there might exist a backdoor that breaks your encryption. I personally recommend using gpg. Make sure you understand GPG enough before using it.

How do I get my recipients to decrypt the message

In public-key encryption, a private key is necessary to decrypt a message. You can think of this as a password. The only sure way you can deliver a password securely is face-to-face. If you're using GPG, you should have a key-signing party with those individuals you want to share messages.
